What Do We Do?

It is difficult to live day-to-day with Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD), the intense phobia of being negatively judged in social situations. We try to alleviate this unfair condition with a free social network designed to make our users comfortable in a follower-free, like-free, anonymous zone. No name, no status, no judgment.

How Do We Do?

  • Anonymity

    Our users are anonymous. No personal information: No real name, no profile picture, not even your location can be displayed. We do this, not to stifle individuality, but to grant users a safe space where they don't have to worry about being judged by their professional & personal lives. Users can trust that what they have to say will be more important than who they are outside of Juntos.

  • No Followers

    Juntos users can not see the amount of followers another user has. We do this because we don't want users chasing popularity, or status through their number of followers. Similarly, we don't want users dictating who they engage with based on popularity. Our users value interactions, not numbers.

  • No Likes

    Our users can not see the amount of likes a different user's post received. We do this, again, to hinder any popularity contests. The number of likes doesn't dictate whether someone's post was more meaningful than your own.

Why Do We Do?

Social anxiety disorder is not nearly as addressed as it should be.
It can be difficult for those with this condition to find jobs, keep jobs, stay in school, or have a social life.

  • 15,000,000 cases of SAD across the US1
  • More than 95% do not report symptoms within a year of them appearing.2
  • 33% experience symptoms for 10 or more years before seeking help.3
  • 5 months is all it takes to start seeing improvement.4
  • 100% of socially anxious people are people. They are more than their condition.
